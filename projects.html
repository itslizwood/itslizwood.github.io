The "Fix It" Project:
Redesign an everyday object.


Two key problems were identified:

1


The remote control has too many buttons and features, making it difficult for one to find exactly what control they are looking for.

2


The remote uses infrared technology that requires the remote to point at the television screen. The feedback is not always instant and can be frustrating for the user.

Principles of Design

Discoverability

The remote control has too many buttons and features, making it difficult for one to find exactly what control they are looking for.

Signifiers

The volume buttons will have a plus and minus to signify to the user how to increase or decrease the volume. Power button will also have a signifier. On the actual touch screen, there will be images to illustrate what action will be performed.

Affordances

The underbelly of the remote is curved, allowing it to lay comfortably in the user's hand.

Constraints

By significantly reducing the amount of buttons on the remote control, this reduces the time it takes for the user to find what they are looking for and reduces the probability of error.

Mapping

The new remote is designed to be easy to handle. Buttons are placed where fingers naturally lie when holding the remote, making the user aware of them.
